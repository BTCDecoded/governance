# Bitcoin Commons Compact

**Version 1.0**

---

## I. Purpose

Bitcoin Commons exists to provide a governance model for alternative Bitcoin implementations, grounded in user consent, cryptographic transparency, and proportional accountability. Not by declaration. By demonstration.

---

## II. Legitimacy

Authority in Bitcoin flows from users. Not developers. Not miners. Not institutions.

Four roles participate in Bitcoin's operation:

- **Network Participants** hold and transact bitcoin
- **Node Operators** validate transactions and enforce rules
- **Miners** produce blocks
- **Developers** codify rules into software that Node Operators choose to run

The legitimacy hierarchy runs as follows:

1. Network Participants grant legitimacy by recognizing Bitcoin as money and transacting under its rules
2. Node Operators serve Network Participants by enforcing the rules they recognize, and nothing beyond that
3. Miners comply with the rules Node Operators enforce
4. Developers codify the rules Network Participants have legitimized into software that Node Operators choose to run

Technical implementation follows social consensus. Never the reverse.

---

## III. Principles

**Settlement Purpose**
Bitcoin Commons recognizes Bitcoin's base layer as a monetary settlement layer. Transfer of value. Settlement of obligations. Preservation of monetary integrity over time. No governance model operating under this Compact may act contrary to that purpose.

**Transparency**
Power must be visible. Governance actions must be auditable. No authority may operate without a public record.

**Cryptographic Enforcement**
Where possible, governance constraints are enforced by mechanism, not by trust. The same cryptographic standards Bitcoin applies to consensus apply to Bitcoin Commons governance.

**Exit Rights**
Participation is voluntary. Any user, node operator, or developer may exit at any time without permission. Low exit costs are a feature, not a bug.

**Proportional Change Costs**
Not all changes are equal. Foundational changes require broader consensus and longer deliberation than routine maintenance. The cost of change must be proportional to its consequences.

---

## IV. Governance Reference

The technical enforcement of these principles is specified in the Bitcoin Commons governance specification in this repository (for example [GOVERNANCE.md](GOVERNANCE.md)): multisig thresholds, timelocks, cryptographic audit trails, and amendment procedures. This Compact is upstream of it. The governance specification serves this Compact, not the reverse.

---

## V. Amendment Process

This Compact may be amended. Permanence is not the goal. Proportionality is.

Amendments to this document require the highest governance threshold defined in the governance specification, equivalent to a constitutional change. Amendments must be proposed publicly, deliberated openly, and adopted through the defined process.

Version numbers track all changes. No amendment is silent. No amendment is retroactive.

---

## VI. Relationship to Bitcoin

Bitcoin Commons does not replace Bitcoin. The only thing being challenged is the claim that any single implementation or group of developers holds exclusive legitimate stewardship over Bitcoin's evolution. That claim has never been formally granted by users.

---

## VII. Adoption

This Compact has no founding authority. It means nothing until users adopt it. No individual or group behind this document has special standing.

Adoption means recognizing these principles as the basis on which Bitcoin Commons operates. It does not confer membership, voting rights, or obligations beyond that recognition.

Adoption is cryptographically expressible. It is what it has always been in Bitcoin: a choice users make, individually, by running software that reflects their values.
